Job Opportunities

Applications are invited for a software developer or software-oriented postdoc position, to join a team of in total 9 postdocs and PhD students to work on the AARTFAAC project. AARTFAAC is a project to extend the LOFAR radio telescope with an all-sky monitor operating in the 30&emdash;80 MHz band, funded through an ERC Advanced Investigator Grant to Prof. Wijers (University of Amsterdam), and to exploit both LOFAR and this extension to study extreme radio transients observationally and theoretically. It will be executed jointly by the University of Amsterdam and ASTRON Netherlands Institute for Radio Astronomy. The project aims to open up new discovery space for very rare transient sources by imaging all the sky, all the time, and to break new ground in the realm of fast, near-real time wide field radio imaging.

For the postdoc/developer position we require an MSc or PhD in Computer Science, Astronomy, or closely related field, and affinity with cuting-edge computer applications to practical Science. Good knowledge is required of C++, Python, and Unix, and some experience in designing and developing software in the context of an astronomical or applied-science environment. The candidate must be flexible in working with colleagues in an environment of changing requirements as the project progresses, and a team player. Salary is on pay scales 9 or 10 of UvA, i.e., in the range €35,000&endash;52,000 per year gross with good benefits.

The position is open to people from non-traditional career tracks, and will start as soon as possible. Evaluation of candidates will start on October 1 and continue until the positions are filled. Candidates should send a motivation letter, CV, and 2-3 letters of recommendation to secr-astro-science@uva.nl. Further inquiries about the positions can be made with Prof. Ralph Wijers (Ralph.Wijers@uva.nl) or Dr. John Swinbank (swinbank@transientskp.org).
